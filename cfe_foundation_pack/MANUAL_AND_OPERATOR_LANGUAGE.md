# Campaign Finance Engine (CFE) — Manual and Operator Language

## Manual voice principles
The manual should feel:
- serious
- calm
- plainspoken
- strategically literate
- useful to working campaign people
- clear about what outputs mean and how to act on them

It should explain:
- what the module is
- why it matters
- how to use it
- what a healthy range looks like
- what weak or risky patterns look like
- what to do next

## Manual intro language
“Campaign Finance Engine is designed to answer a practical question campaigns confront every day: what are we trying to fund, when do those costs hit, and are we doing enough to support the plan? It is not just a record of receipts and spending. It is a budget-path and finance execution system built to help campaigns fund real operating plans with discipline.”

## Budget Builder help language
“This module is where the campaign turns intention into a real spending plan. A budget is not just a topline number. It is a set of commitments that will hit at different times and create different levels of pressure on cash. Build the budget honestly. Mark what is required, what is optional, and what can wait. A clean budget here makes every downstream output stronger.”

## Spend Timeline help language
“The spend timeline matters because campaigns do not fail only when they lack money in the abstract. They fail when money is not available at the moment it is needed. Use this view to see when cash pressure peaks, when reserve matters most, and which spending windows require fundraising to land early rather than late.”

## Benchmark help language
“Benchmarks are not destiny. They are a realism layer. Use them to understand what similar campaigns have historically needed to raise and spend, and how your current plan compares. A plan that differs from history is not automatically wrong, but it should usually be different for a reason, not by accident.”

## Funding Path help language
“The finance path translates budget pressure into raise targets. This is the engine’s answer to the question: what must be raised, and by when, for the selected campaign plan to remain workable? The most important thing here is not just the total target. It is the timing of the target and the gap between safe funding and current pace.”

## Planned vs Actual help language
“This view is meant to answer the question campaigns ask when numbers slip: why are we behind? A campaign can miss its path because activity did not happen, because asks were too weak, because follow-up lagged, because events underperformed, or because money arrived slower than expected. This module helps separate cause from symptom.”

## Donor Intelligence help language
“Donor intelligence shows who is funding the campaign and from where. This is useful for more than curiosity. It helps identify overreliance, white space, coalition depth, and whether the campaign’s financial support is broadening or narrowing over time. Treat these patterns as strategic signals, not as rigid labels.”

## Bridge help language
“The field-to-finance bridge exists to keep the campaign grounded. Field plans create real cost demand. Finance plans determine whether those demands are safely supportable. The bridge is intentionally narrow so each system stays stable. Use it to inform timing and affordability, not to blur the two engines together.”

## Manual warning interpretation language
“When the engine raises a caution or warning, the most important question is not whether the label feels severe. The important question is whether the explanation matches what the campaign is actually experiencing and whether the recommended action is practical. Warnings are meant to support decision-making, not replace it.”
