# Campaign Finance Engine (CFE) — Manual and Operator Language

## Manual voice principles
The manual should feel:
- serious
- calm
- plainspoken
- strategically literate
- useful to working campaign people
- clear about what outputs mean and how to act on them

Every manual section should explain:
- what the module is
- why it matters
- how to use it
- what healthy looks like
- what weak or risky patterns look like
- what to do next

## Global manual introduction
“Campaign Finance Engine is designed to answer a practical question campaigns confront every day: what are we trying to fund, when do those costs hit, and are we doing enough to support the plan? It is not just a record of receipts and spending. It is a budget-path and finance execution system built to help campaigns fund real operating plans with discipline.”

## Race Setup module copy
### What this module is
“This is where the campaign defines the environment it is operating in: office, district, timing, filing cadence, and campaign condition. Those inputs matter because funding pressure and benchmark realism depend heavily on race type and calendar structure.”

### How to use it
“Set this up carefully and keep it current. Errors here travel downstream into benchmarks, timing, and reporting.”

### What healthy looks like
“A healthy setup has a clean calendar, clear office context, and a selected scenario that reflects the real campaign plan rather than an aspirational placeholder.”

## Budget Builder module copy
### What this module is
“This module is where the campaign turns intention into a real spending plan. A budget is not just a topline number. It is a set of commitments that will hit at different times and create different levels of pressure on cash.”

### How to use it
“Build the budget honestly. Mark what is required, what is optional, and what can wait. A clean budget here makes every downstream output stronger.”

### What healthy looks like
“A healthy budget distinguishes core program needs from optional enhancements, uses realistic line items, and avoids hiding pressure inside vague buckets.”

### What weak looks like
“A weak budget is padded in some places, too vague in others, and unrealistic about when costs will hit.”

## Spend Timeline module copy
### What this module is
“The spend timeline matters because campaigns do not fail only when they lack money in the abstract. They fail when money is not available at the moment it is needed.”

### How to use it
“Use this view to see when cash pressure peaks, when reserve matters most, and which spending windows require fundraising to land early rather than late.”

### Healthy interpretation
“A healthy timeline shows that major commitments are visible early enough for the campaign to respond before they become emergencies.”

## Benchmark module copy
### What this module is
“Benchmarks are a realism layer. They help the campaign understand what similar races have historically needed to raise and spend, and how the current plan compares.”

### How to use it
“A plan that differs from history is not automatically wrong, but it should usually be different for a reason, not by accident.”

### Healthy interpretation
“Healthy use of benchmarks treats them as context and discipline, not as a substitute for judgment.”

## Funding Path module copy
### What this module is
“The finance path translates budget pressure into raise targets. This is the engine’s answer to the question: what must be raised, and by when, for the selected plan to remain workable?”

### What matters most
“The most important thing here is not just the total target. It is the timing of that target and the gap between safe funding and current pace.”

### How to act on it
“When the path is under pressure, focus first on the next real checkpoint rather than arguing abstractly about the final total.”

## Channel Targets module copy
### What this module is
“This module shows how the campaign expects to cover the path: through call time, meetings, events, online work, committee activity, or other channels.”

### Healthy interpretation
“A healthy channel plan is diversified enough that one soft week in one channel does not destabilize the full path.”

## Finance Operations module copy
### What this module is
“This is where the campaign tracks the work that actually produces money. Targets without activity discipline are wishful thinking.”

### How to use it
“Schedule the work, complete the work, record the outcomes, and follow up faster than the campaign thinks it needs to.”

### Healthy interpretation
“A healthy operation has clear owners, realistic goals, and fast follow-up on live commitments.”

## Planned vs Actual module copy
### What this module is
“This view is meant to answer the question campaigns ask when numbers slip: why are we behind?”

### Interpretation guidance
“A campaign can miss its path because activity did not happen, because asks were too weak, because follow-up lagged, because events underperformed, or because money arrived slower than expected. This module helps separate cause from symptom.”

## Donor Intelligence module copy
### What this module is
“Donor intelligence shows who is funding the campaign and from where. This is useful for more than curiosity. It helps identify overreliance, white space, coalition depth, and whether the campaign’s financial support is broadening or narrowing over time.”

### Interpretation guidance
“Treat these patterns as strategic signals, not as rigid labels.”

## Bridge module copy
### What this module is
“The field-to-finance bridge exists to keep the campaign grounded. Field plans create real cost demand. Finance plans determine whether those demands are safely supportable.”

### Why it is narrow
“The bridge is intentionally narrow so each system stays stable. Use it to inform timing and affordability, not to blur the two engines together.”

## Warning interpretation copy
“When the engine raises a caution or warning, the most important question is not whether the label feels severe. The important question is whether the explanation matches what the campaign is actually experiencing and whether the recommended action is practical.”

## Unknown-data interpretation copy
“Unknown is not a flaw by itself. Unknown means the system is refusing to pretend it knows something it does not. Use review queues to improve the highest-impact unknowns first.”
