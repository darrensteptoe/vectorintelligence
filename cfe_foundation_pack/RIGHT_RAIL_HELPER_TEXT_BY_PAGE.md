# RIGHT_RAIL_HELPER_TEXT_BY_PAGE

This file contains right-rail teaching text and interpretation notes for major pages. The right rail should feel like a compact operating coach: dense, calm, useful, never gimmicky.

---

## Finance Path Dashboard — Right Rail

### Rail title
**How to read this page**

### Rail body
This page shows whether the campaign’s active plan is financially supportable under current conditions. It is not enough to read the gross target alone. Focus on four things:
- the next funding checkpoint,
- current reserve condition,
- the gap to safe funding,
- and whether current activity is actually carrying the path.

A campaign can look active and still be fragile. The key test is whether money is arriving before pressure points, not merely whether the campaign can point to recent effort.

### Rail note: Good discipline
When the page turns stronger, protect the margin before approving new optional costs.

### Rail note: Bad habit
Do not treat pledges or hoped-for event yield as interchangeable with usable cash.

## Budget Builder — Right Rail

### Rail title
**What this page is for**

### Rail body
Use this page to define the campaign you are actually trying to fund. A finance path can only be as honest as the budget beneath it. The point is not perfect foresight. The point is to make the campaign’s major cost structure visible, phase-aware, and strategically legible.

### Rail note
Missing categories create fake optimism. Optional categories hidden inside the core plan create fake pressure.

## Spend Timeline — Right Rail

### Rail title
**What matters here**

### Rail body
This page is about timing. Ask not only how much the campaign needs, but when those dollars need to exist to keep the plan safe. Sharp pressure often appears before leadership feels it.

### Rail note
The relevant month is often the month before the visible problem, not the month of the problem itself.

## Historical Benchmarks — Right Rail

### Rail title
**How to use history well**

### Rail body
Use benchmarks to test realism, not to replace strategy. A campaign may intentionally sit above or below historical norms, but it should be able to explain why and demonstrate a path that supports the choice.

### Rail note
History is a reference band, not a command.

## Channel Plan — Right Rail

### Rail title
**What to look for**

### Rail body
This page should answer where the required money is expected to come from. Healthy plans distribute responsibility across real channels and real operators. Weak plans hide wishful thinking in one oversized event or one unrealistic online assumption.

### Rail note
If a channel is carrying a large share of the path, ask whether it has the activity and ownership to justify that load.

## Finance Operations — Right Rail

### Rail title
**What counts as progress**

### Rail body
This page is about production, not busyness. Meetings on a calendar do not fund a campaign. Completed asks, disciplined follow-up, converted pledges, and received dollars do.

### Rail note
Missed follow-up is often hidden path loss.

## Donor Intelligence — Right Rail

### Rail title
**How to interpret donor patterns**

### Rail body
Read donor intelligence as strategy guidance. Look for concentration, breadth, local strength, outside dependence, and underdeveloped host terrain. Do not overclaim social certainty from imperfect classification.

### Rail note
The right question is not “who are our donors” in the abstract. It is “what does our donor base suggest about our finance structure and next openings?”

## Expenditure Intelligence — Right Rail

### Rail title
**What this page should prevent**

### Rail body
This page helps the campaign notice when spending is drifting away from its stated strategy. The goal is to catch distortion early: too much overhead, too much consultant gravity, too little protection for field or persuasion, or too many one-off costs carrying strategic weight they do not deserve.

### Rail note
Spend should reflect strategy, not simply habit.

## Risk Console — Right Rail

### Rail title
**How to use warnings well**

### Rail body
Warnings are not meant to create panic. They are meant to improve timing. Start with the trigger, then identify what part of the campaign it threatens, then act on the most controllable fix.

### Rail note
The best warning is the one the campaign uses early enough to avoid a worse one later.

---

## Reports — Right Rail

### Rail title
**What a good report should do**

### Rail body
A good report should tell the reader what condition the campaign is in, why that condition exists, what it threatens, and what should happen next. It should reduce confusion, not create elegant vagueness.

### Rail note
If a report sounds polished but leaves the next move unclear, it is not finished.

## Manual Page — Right Rail

### Rail title
**How to use this manual**

### Rail body
This manual is meant to teach judgment, not just define labels. Use it to understand what the engine is trying to show you, what the terms mean in practice, and how to distinguish healthy, weak, and risky conditions without reading false precision into the data.
