# Campaign Finance Engine (CFE) — Report Section Language

## Executive summary block templates

### Strong status
“The campaign remains on a workable funding path for the selected plan. Current activity and expected receipts are generally aligned with the next spending window, though the margin for error is not unlimited.”

### Mixed status
“The campaign is still within reach of the active funding path, but the margin for error has narrowed. Recent activity has not fully matched target, and upcoming costs will be harder to cover cleanly unless near-term finance work improves.”

### Weak status
“The campaign is currently behind the active funding path. The issue is not only the topline gap, but the timing of that gap relative to upcoming commitments. Without stronger near-term performance or plan adjustment, the current budget will become harder to support safely.”

## Current condition templates

### Pace summary
“For this period, the campaign targeted [TARGET] and has brought in [ACTUAL], reaching [ATTAINMENT]% of the target so far.”

### Reserve summary
“The reserve position is currently [STATUS]. That means the campaign [HAS / DOES NOT HAVE] a comfortable cushion heading into the next spending window.”

### Activity summary
“The campaign completed [COMPLETED] of [PLANNED] planned finance activities this period. That completion rate matters because the current gap appears tied not only to ask quality, but to activity volume itself.”

## Interpretation blocks

### Behind pace because activity lagged
“The primary issue this period appears to be activity volume. The campaign did not complete enough of the planned finance work to give the path a fair chance to succeed. That points first to scheduling, discipline, and follow-up rather than to a purely structural fundraising ceiling.”

### Behind pace because conversion lagged
“The campaign generated activity, but the conversion from activity into money was softer than needed. In practical terms, the issue is less about whether the campaign is working and more about whether the work is sharp enough, targeted enough, and followed through aggressively enough.”

### Budget pressure interpretation
“The most important pressure point is not the total budget in the abstract. It is the timing of the next major commitments. A campaign can feel solvent on paper and still become operationally constrained if money is not in place before those costs arrive.”

### Donor-base interpretation
“The donor base appears [BROAD / NARROW / MIXED]. That is strategically important because a narrow donor base can still generate money in the short run, but it usually leaves the campaign more vulnerable to fatigue, delay, and concentration risk.”

## Risk paragraph templates

### Field affordability
“The selected field posture is not just a strategic preference. It is a timed cost commitment. Under the current funding path, that commitment is [SAFE / TIGHT / NOT YET SAFE], which should affect decisions about hiring, scale, and pacing.”

### Event weakness
“The event program is not currently carrying the amount of the plan assigned to it. That does not mean events should be abandoned, but it does mean the campaign should stop assuming they will solve a broader pacing problem on their own.”

### Outside-money concentration
“A large share of current support is coming from outside the district or outside the campaign’s core geographic base. That can be useful, but it also means the campaign should be careful not to mistake network depth elsewhere for broad local finance strength.”

## Recommended action templates

### Immediate actions
- “Increase candidate call time volume over the next two weeks and narrow the ask list to higher-probability targets.”
- “Push immediate follow-up on unresolved commitments already in the pipeline before adding new lower-probability asks.”
- “Delay optional spending until the next funding checkpoint improves.”
- “Treat the next event as a targeted yield exercise, not a symbolic calendar item.”
- “Do not expand the selected field posture until reserve status returns to a safer range.”
