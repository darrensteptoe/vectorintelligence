# Report Content Pack

## Tone rules for reports
- Crisp, serious, readable.
- Never bloated.
- Each section should answer: what is true, why it matters, what to do next.
- Do not bury risk inside decorative prose.
- Candidate-facing versions should be simpler but not softer on substance.

## Weekly Finance Memo
### Cover summary block
**Weekly Finance Memo**

This memo summarizes the campaign’s current funding path, finance activity, and near-term risk. It is designed to help the finance team and senior campaign leadership focus on what matters most in the next seven days.

### Executive summary template
The campaign remains [status] against the active funding path. Current performance suggests the team is [on pace / slightly behind / materially behind / ahead of pace] relative to this period’s target. The most important near-term question is whether the campaign can [hold current pace / recover pace / safely expand / protect reserve] before the next major checkpoint.

### Budget health section
**Budget Health**
The active campaign budget remains [credible / somewhat aggressive / materially aggressive] relative to comparable campaigns. The core issue is not only total size. It is whether the timing of the plan remains supportable under the current fundraising path.

### Pace section
**Pace and Production**
The campaign’s current raise pace is [status]. This week’s result should be interpreted alongside activity completion, pledge conversion, and expected deposits. A soft week can be absorbed if follow-up is strong. A soft week combined with weak execution is more serious.

### Activity section
**Finance Activity**
The campaign completed [x]% of planned finance activity this period. The strongest activity channel was [channel]. The weakest channel was [channel]. The practical question is whether the completed work is sufficient to support the next checkpoint, not just whether the calendar looked full.

### Risk summary language
**Risk Summary**
The main risks this week are [risk 1], [risk 2], and [risk 3]. None of these are theoretical. Each one has a direct effect on either cash timing, reserve protection, or the campaign’s ability to carry planned commitments without strain.

### Recommended actions block
**Recommended Actions for Next Week**
1. Pull forward high-probability follow-up before adding new lower-confidence asks.
2. Protect reserve before approving additional optional spending.
3. Treat the next checkpoint as an operating deadline, not a suggestion.

## Candidate Brief
### Opening block
**Candidate Finance Brief**

This brief is designed to keep the candidate focused on the finance work that matters most this week. It is not meant to explain every detail of the budget. It is meant to clarify priorities.

### Candidate summary template
The campaign is currently [status] against the active finance path. The most important goal this week is to secure [amount or number of commitments] through [top channels]. The strongest use of candidate time is [call time / major donor follow-up / event preparation / one-on-one asks].

### What the candidate needs to know
- This week’s raise target: [amount]
- Most important ask block: [description]
- Highest-priority follow-up: [description]
- Event that needs attention: [description]
- Main caution: [description]

### Candidate caution language
- “The campaign can still recover this month, but only if follow-up is treated as seriously as fresh asks.”
- “Do not assume pledged dollars are equivalent to received dollars.”
- “This week’s result matters less than whether the campaign is staying disciplined into the next checkpoint.”

## Finance Committee Brief
### Opening block
**Finance Committee Brief**

This brief is designed to help finance committee members understand where the campaign stands, what support is needed now, and which commitments should be treated as urgent.

### Summary template
The campaign is currently [status] against the active finance path. Committee support is especially important in [area / host program / major donor follow-up / event turnout]. The committee can be most useful over the next period by helping convert [type of target] into real commitments or receipts.

### Committee accountability section
**Committee Performance**
Assigned asks: [count]
Completed asks: [count]
Commitments secured: [count or amount]
Follow-up still needed: [count]

### Committee action language
- “The campaign does not need a larger committee on paper. It needs faster movement from current assignments.”
- “Host and connector activity matters most when it produces real introductions and actual follow-up, not just attendance.”
- “The next period is a good time to prioritize quality of asks over sheer volume.”

## Budget Health Report
### Opening summary
**Budget Health Report**

This report evaluates whether the campaign’s full operating plan is currently supportable under the active finance path. It focuses on timing, reserve protection, category pressure, and the difference between a budget that looks plausible and a budget that is actually cash-safe.

### Summary template
The campaign’s budget is currently [stable / supportable with caution / under strain / in redline]. The key issue is [timing pressure / weak reserve / aggressive category load / slow raise pace / field ramp risk]. At current pace, the campaign can safely support [description] but should treat [description] as conditional.

### Category pressure language
- “This category is not inherently too large, but it is arriving too early relative to the finance path.”
- “This cost is strategically valid, but it should not crowd out required operating protection.”
- “This category now deserves leadership review because it is beginning to shape tradeoffs elsewhere in the plan.”

### Greenlight/caution/redline copy
- **Greenlight**: The campaign can proceed without unusual strain under the active assumptions.
- **Caution**: The campaign can proceed, but only with tighter timing discipline and closer checkpoint management.
- **Redline**: The campaign should not proceed with this commitment until the funding path materially improves.

## Filing Snapshot
### Opening block
**Filing Period Snapshot**

This report is designed for the period leading into a public filing or major disclosure checkpoint. It focuses on what is likely to be reflected in the filing, what still sits in the pledge or follow-up pipeline, and how the resulting number may compare to expectations.

### Summary template
The campaign is heading into the filing period with [status]. The likely reported result will depend on whether [key expected receipts] land in time. The more important issue is not only the public number, but what that number means for the campaign’s next operating phase.

### Filing caution language
- “Do not confuse expected money with filed money.”
- “A better public number can help, but only if the underlying path is genuinely improving.”
- “A soft filing can be survivable if the campaign has a credible recovery path immediately after.”

## Donor Intelligence Memo
### Opening block
**Donor Intelligence Memo**

This memo summarizes the shape of the campaign’s donor base, including geography, occupation and industry patterns, concentration, and local-versus-external support. It is best used to sharpen finance strategy, not just describe donors for curiosity’s sake.

### Summary template
The donor base currently appears [broad / concentrated / locally grounded / externally dependent / mixed]. The most important insight is that support is strongest in [geography / sector / donor type], while [geography / sector / donor type] remains comparatively underdeveloped.

### Interpretation guidance
- “A broad donor map with shallow dollar depth suggests expansion opportunity.”
- “A strong high-dollar cluster can be valuable, but it should not become the only working engine.”
- “Occupation and industry mix should be treated as directional intelligence, not personal biography.”

## Leadership Risk Report
### Opening block
**Leadership Risk Report**

This report is intended for the campaign manager, senior consultant, or leadership group. It focuses on what could materially disrupt execution if not addressed in the near term.

### Summary template
The campaign’s main near-term risks are [risk list]. These risks matter because they affect the campaign’s ability to carry planned costs, protect reserve, and make timing-sensitive decisions with confidence.

### Risk paragraph templates
#### Pace risk
The campaign’s current raise pace is not yet strong enough to comfortably support the active plan. This is manageable if corrected early, but it should not be normalized.

#### Reserve risk
Reserve protection has weakened to a point where ordinary slippage could begin to affect operating decisions. The right response is not panic. It is discipline.

#### Field-funding risk
The selected field scenario may be strategically sound, but it is not yet fully supported under the current finance path. Expansion decisions should remain conditional until funding coverage improves.
