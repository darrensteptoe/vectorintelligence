# MANUAL_DENSITY_AND_TEACHING_STANDARD.md

## Purpose
The CFE manual must teach, not decorate.

It should feel like the finance sibling of the FPE manual: a serious operational guide that helps campaigns understand what they are looking at, what good looks like, what bad looks like, and how to make decisions.

## Manual standard
Every major page or module should explain:
1. What this module is.
2. Why it matters.
3. What the main metrics mean.
4. What strong vs weak conditions usually look like.
5. What operators should do with this information.
6. What the module can and cannot prove.

## Teaching expectations
The manual must include:
- plain-language explanations
- campaign-world interpretation
- range guidance where appropriate
- caution against false certainty
- action guidance
- examples of how a strong operator would read the page

## Tone rules
- Serious, not academic.
- Helpful, not condescending.
- Dense, but not bloated.
- Campaign-literate, not software-literate.
- Confident, not theatrical.

## Required manual surfaces
- page intro for every top-level page
- card intro for major summary cards
- metric explainers for all major engine outputs
- warning explainers
- empty-state teaching copy
- workflow guidance for budgeting, pathing, call time, events, donor analysis, and reporting

## Example manual pattern
### What this page is for
This page helps you understand whether the campaign’s current fundraising program is sufficient to support the budget it is trying to run.

### How to read it
Start with the total funding requirement, then review the next checkpoint, then look at reserve status. A campaign can look healthy on the total number while still being exposed in the next spend window.

### What a strong condition looks like
A strong condition usually means the campaign is not only on pace overall, but is also entering the next major spend period with enough cash flexibility to protect the plan.

### What a weak condition looks like
A weak condition usually means the campaign is relying on future assumptions that have not yet been validated by actual finance activity.

### What to do next
If reserve pressure is rising, focus first on near-term channel production and delay nonessential commitments before expanding the spend plan.
