Platform

The analytical framework behind the work.

The platform page exists to show that Vector’s analysis comes from a real modeling framework, not from ad hoc spreadsheet improvisation. It is still a managed system, but the underlying logic is deliberate and consistent.

In practical terms, this means the same assumptions, definitions, and decision rules carry through the engagement instead of changing from memo to memo.

01
Core Principles
Deterministic by Design

Same inputs should produce the same outputs. That keeps the system readable and keeps disagreement focused on assumptions rather than mystery behavior.

Scenario-Ready

The platform is structured to test conditions, not just summarize them. Clients can ask what changes if key variables move.

Operationally Relevant

The model is designed to connect strategy to things teams can actually adjust: staffing, timing, targeting, budget, and pressure points.

02
What the Framework Can Ingest

Campaign side: voter universes, turnout baselines, expected support levels, contact rates, volunteer assumptions, organizer capacity, and budget allocations.

Policy side: current legal framework, bill text, amendments, committee activity, lobbying registrations, campaign finance relationships, and demographic context.

The point is not to capture every possible input. It is to include the inputs that materially improve strategic interpretation.

  • Consistent definitions
    Terms and thresholds are held steady across the engagement.
  • Traceable assumptions
    Outputs can be walked back to specific decisions and inputs.
  • Readable outputs
    The model exists to clarify judgment, not obscure it.
