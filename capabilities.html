Capabilities

What Vector can actually do for a client.

Capabilities are presented plainly here on purpose. The firm is small. The systems are focused. The work centers on strategic modeling, policy analysis, and turning complicated environments into decisions a team can use.

Vector is not trying to be a generic consultancy, an all-in-one campaign vendor, or a software company pretending to be something else. The offer is narrower than that, and stronger for it.

01
Campaign Analysis
Win Path Modeling

Estimate the vote threshold, the current expected path, and the remaining gap under explicit assumptions.

Field Program Feasibility

Test whether planned organizer capacity, volunteer production, and contact rates are enough to hit the modeled target.

Pacing & Reality Checks

Compare actual campaign outputs to the assumptions the plan depends on, so drift is identified early rather than after the fact.

Persuasion & GOTV Modeling

Model how persuasion, turnout lift, and undecided allocation interact in the same race rather than as separate thought experiments.

Scenario Testing

Explore what changes if staffing, budget, timing, turnout, or baseline support assumptions move up or down.

Budget Efficiency Review

Evaluate which tactics are likely to close the gap most efficiently under real campaign constraints.

02
Policy Analysis

Current law analysis. Before evaluating a bill, Vector can establish the baseline legal framework by reviewing the relevant statutory, constitutional, and regulatory environment.

Bill interpretation. Proposed legislation is analyzed to determine what it is designed to do, what programs or authorities it creates or modifies, and where the important practical effects are likely to occur.

Legislative evolution tracking. Bills rarely pass in original form. Amendments, substitutes, and negotiations often matter more than the opening text.

Influence and stakeholder mapping. Legislative dynamics can be interpreted in context using lobbying relationships, campaign finance connections, institutional roles, and coalition structure.
